# NeuroSeal — Investment Details

## The Problem

Hemorrhagic stroke and traumatic intracranial bleeding represent the deadliest and most time-critical medical emergencies. Current interventions — craniotomy, endovascular coiling, external ventricular drain — require neurosurgeons, specialized OR suites, and 45–90 minutes of procedure time from arrival. Every 30 minutes of delay costs ~2 million neurons.

---

## The Opportunity

**Global neurovascular intervention market:** $4.1B in 2024, growing at 8.9% CAGR
**Hemorrhagic stroke incidence (US):** ~800,000 events/year; 40% mortality at 30 days
**TBI hospitalizations (US):** 220,000/year; $76B in annual economic burden
**FDA Breakthrough Device Designation:** Likely applicable — addresses serious/life-threatening condition with no adequate alternative

---

## Product Economics

**NeuroSeal System — Hospital (ICU/Neurology)**
- Pricing model: Capital equipment + disposable delivery kit
- Console: $280,000–$380,000 (one-time, per facility)
- Delivery kit (per procedure): $4,200–$6,800
- COGS estimate: Console ~$85K; kit ~$620
- Gross margin target: 70%+ (console), 85%+ (disposables)

**Addressable Procedure Volume (US):**
- Hemorrhagic stroke: ~130,000 interventional candidates/year
- Severe TBI with surgical indication: ~45,000/year
- **Total: ~175,000 procedures/year at $4,200–$6,800/kit = $735M–$1.19B TAM (disposables alone)**
