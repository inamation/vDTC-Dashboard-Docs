# IronShield — Small Modular Reactor

**Carbon-free baseload nuclear power for the vDTC infrastructure campus at Fort Custer, Battle Creek MI.**

IronShield is a proposed Small Modular Reactor (SMR) project at the Fort Custer site in Battle Creek, Michigan. Designed to provide 50–300 MW of carbon-free, always-on baseload power — sized initially for the Luminary data center campus and expandable to serve municipal and industrial loads.

---

## Technology Selection

vDTC is evaluating SMR vendors against criteria of NRC licensing status, first-of-a-kind timeline, power output range, and cooling footprint:

| Vendor | Technology | Output | NRC Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NuScale Power** | Light water SMR (VOYGR-6) | 6 × 77 MW = 462 MW | NRC SDA approved 2022 |
| **TerraPower (Natrium)** | Sodium-cooled fast reactor + molten salt storage | 345 MW (500 MW burst) | Demonstration underway (WY) |
| **X-energy (Xe-100)** | HTGR pebble bed | 4 × 80 MW = 320 MW | NRC pre-application review |
| **Kairos Power (KP-FHR)** | Fluoride salt-cooled | 140 MW | NRC pre-application review |

**Current lead candidate:** TerraPower Natrium (molten salt storage enables peak-demand buffering for data center spikes)

---

## Site: Fort Custer, Battle Creek MI

- BRAC-converted former Army installation — brownfield/industrial zoning
- Proximity to W.K. Kellogg Airport (BTL) and existing industrial utilities
- Great Lakes water access for cooling (Kalamazoo River watershed)
- Michigan Public Service Commission jurisdiction; NRC 10 CFR Part 52 licensing

---

## Status

> **Current:** Site assessment and NRC licensing process initiated. Vendor engagement ongoing.
>
> **Next:** NRC Early Site Permit (ESP) application — 3–5 year review. Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) initiation.
>
> **Estimated first power:** 2031–2033 (contingent on NRC timeline)
