# IronShield — Investment Details

## Strategic Rationale

Energy is the binding constraint on AI compute. The Luminary data center requires 40–200 MW of reliable, carbon-free baseload power. Commercial utility contracts carry 25–40% price escalation risk over 20-year horizons. Nuclear SMR eliminates fuel cost volatility, achieves zero-carbon LCOE of $65–$85/MWh (vs $110–$160/MWh grid average in data center markets), and provides energy independence for a campus that cannot afford outages.

IronShield is not a utility play — it is infrastructure that directly enables the vDTC Luminary data center and creates a defensible competitive moat for AI compute economics over the next 30 years.

## Risk Factors

- NRC licensing timeline: 3–7 years is standard; TerraPower Natrium first-of-kind is 2025 target
- Public acceptance: Michigan has no recent nuclear construction history; community engagement required
- DOE funding competition: Limited annual capacity in Title XVII program
- Grid interconnect: MISO interconnection queue — 18–24 month study process
