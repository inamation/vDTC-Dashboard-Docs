# Axiom Quantum — Investment Details

## Strategic Rationale

Quantum computing is entering the early commercial era. IBM's 1,000+ qubit Condor and IonQ's trapped-ion systems are demonstrating quantum advantage in narrow optimization and simulation tasks. The window to build quantum infrastructure and application expertise — before the market matures and barriers rise — is now. Axiom Quantum positions vDTC at the intersection of quantum hardware access, Luminary's purpose-built cryogenic hosting environment, and real application domains (drug discovery, cryptography, logistics) where quantum delivers measurable value.

---

## Market Context

**Global quantum computing market:** $1.3B in 2024, growing at 32% CAGR to $12.6B by 2030
**Quantum-as-a-service:** IBM, IonQ, Quantinuum, and Rigetti all have cloud access programs — the market is building toward accessible QPU time
**Quantum-safe cryptography:** NIST PQC standards finalized 2024 (CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium) — every organization must migrate; vDTC's QKD and PQC services address this directly
**Drug discovery:** Pharmaceutical companies spent $238B on R&D in 2023; quantum molecular simulation could reduce target identification timelines by 30–50%

## Competitive Moat

Axiom Quantum's defensible position is not in QPU hardware — IBM and IonQ dominate that. The moat is in:
1. **Luminary co-location:** 40–200 MW carbon-free power + cryogenic-grade facility — no competitor can offer this at IronShield economics
2. **Domain applications:** VQE for vDTC's own drug discovery portfolio gives real-world validation data competitors cannot buy
3. **PQC / QKD:** NIST 2024 mandate creates $8B+ migration market with 5-year urgency window
