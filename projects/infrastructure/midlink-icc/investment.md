# Midlink-ICC — Investment Details

## Development Overview

Midlink-ICC is a 15-story, ~185,000 sq ft mixed-use tower anchoring the vDTC infrastructure campus near the Battle Creek / Fort Custer airport corridor in Michigan. The development is designed to attract aerospace, defense, medical technology, and government contractors — tenant segments with strong retention, credit quality, and mission alignment with vDTC's portfolio.

---

## Market Context

**Battle Creek / Fort Custer corridor:** Home to Fort Custer Industrial Park, W.K. Kellogg Airport (BTL), and established aerospace/defense presence (Lear Corporation, Battle Creek Air National Guard 110th Wing). The corridor is underserved in Class A technology office supply.

**Michigan economic incentives:** MEDC Good Jobs for Michigan program, Renaissance Zone eligibility, and Federal Opportunity Zone designation potential for Fort Custer proximate parcels.

## Risk Factors

- FAA 7460-1 obstruction determination — height and lighting requirements may affect design
- Michigan permitting timeline: estimated 14–22 months for construction permits
- Tenant pre-leasing target: 35% committed before construction financing closes
- IronShield co-development timeline dependency (campus value proposition)
