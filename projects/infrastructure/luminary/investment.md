# Luminary Architecture — Investment Details

## Strategic Position

Luminary is the intersection of two secular trends: explosive AI compute demand and the carbon constraint on new data center development. Every major cloud provider is capacity-constrained and carbon-scrutinized. Luminary offers what none of them can build quickly: dedicated, carbon-free nuclear-powered AI compute with hospital-grade uptime, sovereign from commercial cloud pricing and terms.

The Luminary + IronShield combination creates a 30-year cost advantage in compute economics — the most durable moat in infrastructure.

---

## Market Context

**Global data center market:** $278B in 2023; growing at 10.9% CAGR
**AI compute demand:** NVIDIA estimates GPU cluster demand growing 5× by 2027
**Carbon constraints:** Microsoft, Google, Amazon all have net-zero commitments — carbon-free data center capacity commands 15–25% premium pricing
**Nuclear-powered data center precedent:** Amazon AWS signed deal for 960 MW nuclear-powered data center (Susquehanna, PA, 2024). Microsoft signed 20-year agreement with Constellation Energy for Three Mile Island restart. Luminary is positioned at the leading edge of this wave.

## Revenue Model

### Colocation — Enterprise
- Pricing: $180–$240/kW/month (AI GPU colocation with liquid cooling)
- Occupancy target: 85% (steady state Year 3)
- 40 MW Phase 1 at $210/kW/month × 85% occupancy = **$85.7M/year**

### Managed AI Inference — Service
- Pricing: $2.80–$4.20/GPU-hour (A100 equivalent); $0.95–$1.40/TPU-hour
- Target utilization: 78% at steady state
- Managed GPU fleet: 2,000 A100s → **~$38M/year at steady state**

### Government / Classified
- Pricing: $280–$380/kW/month (SCIF-compliant, isolated cage)
- Target: 5 MW dedicated government wing
- **~$16.8M–$22.8M/year**

### vDTC Internal (Transfer Pricing)
- Internal compute at LCOE-based cost: $68/MWh — **not external revenue, but eliminates cloud spend**
