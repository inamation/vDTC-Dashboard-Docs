# Anatomic AI — Investment Details

## The Problem

Radiology departments face a global shortage: 30% of imaging studies in US community hospitals have delayed reads. Surgical planning relies on manual annotation by radiologists — a 2–4 hour process per case. Medical education programs lack patient-safe high-fidelity simulation. AI has proven capable in narrow imaging tasks but no platform integrates multi-modal segmentation, planning, and language generation into a deployable clinical tool.

---

## The Opportunity

**Global AI in medical imaging market:** $2.4B in 2023, growing at 34% CAGR to $20B by 2030
**Radiology AI TAM:** ~1.2B imaging studies/year in the US; 15%+ CAGR in AI-assisted reads
**Surgical planning AI:** Early market — Stryker, Medtronic, and Zimmer Biomet all have active AI planning initiatives
**Medical education:** 155 US medical schools, 1,100+ residency programs; simulation budgets increasing post-COVID

---

## Revenue Streams

### Radiology AI — SaaS
- Pricing: $0.80–$2.50 per study read (volume tier)
- Target market: 5,500 US hospitals with imaging departments
- 2030 projection: 18M studies/year at $1.20 avg = $21.6M ARR

### Surgical Planning — Per-Case
- Pricing: $380–$680 per pre-operative plan
- Target market: 1.2M surgeries/year with complex access planning
- 2030 projection: 95,000 plans/year at $480 avg = $45.6M ARR

### Medical Education — License
- Pricing: $45,000–$120,000/year per institution
- Target market: 155 medical schools + 400 simulation centers
- 2030 projection: 280 institutions at $72K avg = $20.2M ARR
